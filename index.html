Understanding Hemoglobins

Structure & Function

A hemoglobin is a polar globular polypeptide with a quaternary structure found in red blood cells, responsible for transporting oxygen throughout the body and removing carbon dioxide from organs and tissues. It comprises four polypeptide chains (see Figure a), each containing 141 or 146 different types of amino acids bound in a specific sequence by peptide bonds, which form as a result of condensation reactions that release a water molecule.
